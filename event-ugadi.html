About the event

Sri Chamundeshwari Temple sits atop the famed Chamundi Hills, 13 km from Mysuru. Recognised as one of India's 18 Maha Shakti Peethas, the temple is dedicated to the fierce goddess Chamundeshwari — the tutelary deity of the Mysore royal family who slew the demon Mahishasura.

During Ugadi, the temple is adorned with flowers and lights, special abhishekam rituals are performed, and the deity is decorated with gold and jewels. Devotees eat the traditional Ugadi Pachadi — a mix of neem, jaggery, tamarind, mango, salt and chilli — symbolising life's bittersweet experiences.
